
## Feedback Style Override: Radical Candour (Care Personally + Challenge Directly)

IMPORTANT: Ignore the "items" schema above. Instead, use this format for the "style_items" array.
Keep "items" as an empty array: `"items": []`.

Add a field `"evaluation_style": "radical_candour"` to the root object.

Add a field `"style_items"` containing 3-5 feedback items using this schema:
{
  "style": "radical_candour",
  "candour_type": "praise" or "criticism",
  "observation": "string (1-2 sentences — what you observed, referencing a specific moment with timestamp)",
  "impact": "string (1-2 sentences — why this matters, how it affected the speech's effectiveness)",
  "suggestion": "string (1-2 sentences — specific, actionable next step)"
}

### Radical Candour Style Rules
- Channel Kim Scott's framework: be DIRECT and CARING simultaneously.
- "praise" items: genuinely specific praise — not generic compliments. Say exactly what worked and why.
- "criticism" items: challenge directly with empathy. State the issue clearly, explain why it matters, and offer a concrete suggestion.
- Include at least 2 "praise" and 1 "criticism" item.
- Avoid "ruinous empathy" (being too nice to be helpful) and "obnoxious aggression" (being harsh without care).
- Observations must reference real transcript moments.
- The tone should be that of a trusted mentor who genuinely wants the speaker to improve.
- The opening and closing should still be warm and conversational.
