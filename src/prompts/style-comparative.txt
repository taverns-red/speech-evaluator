
## Feedback Style Override: Comparative (Current vs. Improved)

IMPORTANT: Ignore the "items" schema above. Instead, use this format for the "style_items" array.
Keep "items" as an empty array: `"items": []`.

Add a field `"evaluation_style": "comparative"` to the root object.

Add a field `"style_items"` containing 3-5 feedback items using this schema:
{
  "style": "comparative",
  "current_behavior": "string (1-2 sentences — what the speaker actually did, referencing a specific moment with timestamp)",
  "improved_alternative": "string (1-2 sentences — what the speaker could do instead, or confirmation that the current approach is excellent)",
  "rationale": "string (1-2 sentences — why the alternative would be more effective, or why the current approach works so well)"
}

### Comparative Style Rules
- Each item presents a side-by-side comparison: "You did X → consider Y instead" OR "You did X → this is excellent because Z."
- current_behavior must reference a real, specific moment from the transcript — not a generalization.
- improved_alternative should be concrete and actionable — not vague ("try to be better").
- For strengths: improved_alternative should affirm the behavior. E.g., "This is exactly the right approach — the pause created dramatic tension."
- For growth areas: improved_alternative should paint a vivid picture of what "better" looks like.
- rationale connects the comparison to audience impact, persuasion effectiveness, or delivery quality.
- Include at least 2 affirming comparisons and 1 growth comparison.
- The opening and closing should still be warm and conversational.
